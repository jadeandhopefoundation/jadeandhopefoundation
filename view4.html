Summer Cancer Research Project , Incorporation with University of California, San Francisco As one of the Jade and Hope Foundation’s on-going long-term projects, support Cancer research and help cancer patient with better treatment and quality of life. Working and helping scientific research institute and their innovative physician-scientist to develop new treatment and cures, as well as train and inspire the next generation to follow in their footsteps. Area of interest including research on cancer treatment, such as immunotherapy, chemotherapy, … We are especially interested in helping pancreatic cancer treatment development and pancreatic cancer patient assistance. Pancreatic cancer is one of the most deadly form of cancer with an average patient lifetime of 6-12 months. This project, in which the Jade and Hope Foundation participates through the University of California San Francisco School of Medicine, Helen Diller Family Comprehensive Cancer Center. UCSF has proposed their research funding needs and our foundation has sponsored a research fellowship to new MD or PhD student of UCSF to research the cancer treatment in the summer.
